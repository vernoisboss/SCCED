Buildings and Health

Healthy buildings and shelter have non toxic building materials, are built to withstand natural disasters and are comfortable for the climate of the region
Damp conditions, insufficient daylight, or uncomfortably cold temperatures can all effect health. The increase in extreme weather events due to climate change means more extreme hot, wet and sometimes cold weather patterns that older structures were not designed for.


The 1995 Chicago heat wave was a heat wave which led to 739 heat-related deaths in Chicago over a period of five days.[1] Most of the victims of the heat wave were elderly poor residents of the city, who could not afford air conditioning and did not open windows or sleep outside for fear of crime"

environment

Sustainanble building and re-use is criticle for now and the future.

Resources and energy used in the construction and mateials, keeping them warm or cool and the land used to build all create environmntal impacts.

Re-using older buildings and increasing efficiency reduces GHG from daily operations and minimizes the embodied GHG production compared to a new building.

Using green roofs and vegetation to cool and insulate saves energy costs and reduces GHG emissions. Appropriate landscaping for the climate protects valuable water resources and provides habitat to improve bio-diversity.

Co-locating near services and transit hubs promotes healthier lifestyles and reduces transportation impacts on the environment
